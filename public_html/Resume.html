Below is a summary of my work and education history. I am always happy to talk to people about my experience. I love sharing my successes and challenges with the hope of helping someone else.


Robotics Department, Oregon State University September, 2016 - Current
Position PhD candidate

I currently work on robotic grasping. My work focuses on using expert demonstrations from people to learn intersting strategies for grasping. I believe there is a comprehensive framework that can deal with contact and establish robust grasps from sensory data for grasping objects. I am intersted in using modern control techniques to improve the abilities of robots to grasp a wide range of objects. I am especially interested in the intersection of deep learning and reinforcement learning to achieve complex behaviors.
During my classes, I have had the opportunity to dive into optimization, control theory, planning, and kinematics. Additionally, I have used class projects as an opportunity to attempt to solve real problems with the skills I have learned. I have also enjoyed applying what I have learned to my personal robotics projects.

Rethink Robotics January, 2014 - June, 2016
Position Electromechanical Technician and Manfuacturing Engineer
Between jobs, I travelled for 4 months and discovered a field that fascinated me. I thought Robotics could be an interesting area so I jumped in to a hands on position at Rethink. I loved working with phyiscal, embedded, and software systems while building late stage prototype Sawyer robots. I learned about all three areas and the issues that can arise when trying to build a robot. When the robot was ready for early production, I was converted to a Manufacturing Engineer to improve processes and transfer knowledge to a contract manufacturer.

Spiracur Inc. July, 2012 - April, 2014
Position Manfuacturing Engineer
In my first job, I worked as a manufacturing engineer at a small company. I learned a lot of practical skills like documentation, scheduling, prioritization, and cost efficiency. I spent the majority of my time improving production processes through big and small changes while also maintaining throughput. I used some of my technical skills like 3D modelling to have fixtures made. I also learned how to clearly and concisely explain my decisions. Many of the skills I learned in this job have been the things that stood out most for future employers.
